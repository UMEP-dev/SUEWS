.. _scm_coupled:

==================================================
Coupled Single-Column Model (Research Preview)
==================================================

.. note::
   This capability is a **research preview**. The physics ships in the
   SUEWS core and is exercised by the test suite, but the Python
   interface (:mod:`supy.scm`) may change between releases without a
   deprecation cycle. An interactive companion page with live
   in-browser physics is available at
   `suews.io/preview/scm <https://suews.io/preview/scm/>`_.

Overview
========

Offline, SUEWS (Surface Urban Energy and Water Balance Scheme) consumes
prescribed meteorology: air temperature, humidity and wind are read from
a forcing file, so the atmosphere can never respond to the surface. The
coupled single-column model (SCM) closes this loop. A one-dimensional
atmospheric boundary-layer column runs *inside* the SUEWS timestep
driver: each step the column hands SUEWS the air state at the forcing
height, and SUEWS hands back the turbulent fluxes that drive the column
in return. Radiation, precipitation and pressure remain prescribed, so
the coupled system stays anchored to the observed sky.

With the atmosphere prognostic, quantities that an offline run can only
consume become model *outputs*: the urban heat island, boundary-layer
depth and its seasonal cycle, and the air-temperature response to
surface interventions (cool roofs, anthropogenic-heat scenarios) all
emerge from the surface--air negotiation.

The entire coupled loop executes in the compiled core
(``suews_phys_scm.f95``; loop in ``SUEWS_cal_multitsteps_scm``), reached
through the Rust bridge in a single call. Measured cost is about
0.25 ms per coupled 5-minute step: six coupled column-years complete in
under three minutes on a laptop.

Model formulation
=================

Atmospheric column
------------------

Prognostic potential temperature :math:`\theta`, specific humidity
:math:`q` and horizontal wind :math:`(u, v)` on a stretched grid
(default: first layer 20 m, top 3 km, stretching factor 1.06).
Turbulent fluxes and eddy diffusivities live on cell interfaces.

- **Unstable conditions** — first-order non-local K-profile closure
  within the diagnosed boundary layer (Troen and Mahrt, 1986), with the
  counter-gradient correction and turbulent Prandtl number of Holtslag
  and Boville (1993).
- **Stable conditions and the free atmosphere** — local Blackadar
  mixing-length closure with a Richardson-number stability function.
  Two forms are available: a sharp cut-off
  :math:`f = (1 - Ri/Ri_c)^2` with :math:`Ri_c = 0.2`, and a long-tail
  form :math:`f = 1/(1 + 10\,Ri)`. The sharp form reproduces the GABLS1
  large-eddy-simulation ensemble; the long tail is required for
  multi-season runs (see :ref:`scm_closure_choices`).
- **Boundary-layer height** — bulk Richardson number method (critical
  value 0.25) with the Vogelezang and Holtslag (1996) shear term
  (:math:`100\,u_*^2`) and a Troen--Mahrt convective thermal excess.
- **Numerics** — backward-Euler implicit vertical diffusion in
  conservative flux form (column integrals conserved exactly up to the
  prescribed surface flux), with the surface flux pair
  (:math:`\overline{w\theta}_s`, :math:`\overline{wq}_s`) and friction
  velocity supplied by SUEWS each substep (default: five 60 s column
  substeps per 5-minute SUEWS step).

Coupling sequence
-----------------

Per SUEWS timestep:

1. the column state is interpolated to the measurement height and
   written into the forcing record (air temperature, relative humidity,
   wind speed);
2. SUEWS advances one step and returns :math:`Q_H`, :math:`Q_E` and
   :math:`u_*`;
3. the kinematic fluxes drive the column substeps, followed by wind
   nudging towards a logarithmic profile anchored at the observed speed
   (a one-dimensional column cannot generate the synoptic pressure
   gradient), optional ventilation, and the optional synoptic anchor.

Ventilation and the rural companion
-----------------------------------

A fixed column over a city is not a closed system: advection replaces
urban-heated air with upstream air on a time scale
:math:`\tau = L/U` (roughly an hour for central London). Without this
exchange the modelled city warms without bound, because an urban surface
maintains a positive sensible heat flux through the night — the same
reason the historical SUEWS-CBL (BLUEWS) slab scheme ran daytime-only
with daily re-initialisation (Onomura et al., 2015).

The SCM represents ventilation with a **background atmosphere**,
typically generated by a companion run over a rural configuration
(:func:`~supy.scm.make_rural_config`), whose hourly profiles the urban
column relaxes towards on the advective time scale.

.. _scm_closure_choices:

Multi-season closure choices
----------------------------

Two findings from the multi-year experiments, stated as configuration
guidance:

- the sharp Richardson cut-off decouples the winter stable boundary
  layer and allows unbounded near-surface cooling — the classic runaway
  that pushed operational weather models towards long-tail stability
  functions. Use ``stable_fn=1`` for any run crossing seasons.
- a closed rural column has no large-scale advection and drifts cold
  through winter. The synoptic anchor (``obs_anchor_tau``, typically
  86400 s) relaxes the whole column towards a profile anchored at the
  observed air state; with a one-day time scale the column keeps its own
  diurnal cycle while following the regional air mass. The urban column
  remains fully prognostic.

Validation
==========

All metrics below are archived, with provenance, under
``test/fixtures/scm/`` in the repository; the figures are reproduced on
the `preview page <https://suews.io/preview/scm/>`_.

- **GABLS1 stable boundary layer** (Beare et al., 2006; Cuxart et al.,
  2006) — boundary-layer depth 168 m (stress-based) / 198 m (bulk-Ri)
  against the 150--250 m LES ensemble range; friction velocity
  0.274 m |s^-1|; supergeostrophic low-level jet at 184 m. The long-tail
  variant reproduces the documented over-deepening.
- **Convective growth** — within 4.0 % of the Tennekes (1973) analytic
  entrainment law over six hours (the slab formulation used for the
  in-browser demonstration: 0.3 %). The flux-profile entrainment ratio
  (0.06 against the LES consensus 0.15--0.25; Sullivan et al., 1998) is
  a known limitation of first-order K-profile schemes without an
  explicit entrainment closure (Noh et al., 2003).
- **Coupled against observations** — five July 2012 days over central
  London (KCL site): air-temperature RMSE 3.7 K (3.0 K daytime / 4.5 K
  nighttime), correlation 0.73. The air-temperature observations are
  withheld after initialisation; radiation, precipitation and pressure
  remain prescribed, and wind is nudged towards the observed speed.
- **Multi-year climatology** — three coupled years (urban plus anchored
  rural companion): nocturnal screen-level heat island 3.2 K on
  average; urban daily-maximum boundary-layer depth 1549 m (JJA) versus
  574 m (DJF).

Usage
=====

.. code-block:: python

   from pathlib import Path

   import supy as sp
   from supy.scm import run_scm, make_rural_config, make_background
   from supy.data_model import SUEWSConfig

   # runs as-is on the bundled sample dataset; point the config and
   # forcing at your own site to go further
   config = SUEWSConfig.from_yaml(
       Path(sp.__file__).parent / "sample_data" / "sample_config.yml"
   )
   df_state, df_forcing = sp.load_sample_data()
   window = df_forcing.loc["2012-07-22":"2012-07-26"]

   # rural companion provides the ventilating background air
   rural = run_scm(make_rural_config(config), window, snapshot_every_h=1.0)

   # urban column, ventilated, fully prognostic
   res = run_scm(config, window, background=make_background(rural.snapshots))
   res.output["SUEWS"]["QH"]      # standard SUEWS output
   res.diagnostics["tair_mod"]    # prognostic air temperature

:func:`~supy.scm.run_scm` always returns an
:class:`~supy.scm.ScmResult` with ``output`` (standard SUEWS output),
``diagnostics`` (per-step column diagnostics: air temperature, humidity
and wind at the forcing height, boundary-layer depth, kinematic fluxes,
ventilation time scale), ``snapshots`` (``None`` unless
``snapshot_every_h`` is set) and ``state_json``. All parameter
overrides are validated against finite/range contracts mirrored by the
Fortran side. For multi-year experiments from a single observed year,
:func:`~supy.scm.tile_forcing` recycles the forcing across calendar
years (the input must cover exactly one calendar year on a regular
step; the leap day is handled, and impossible tilings are rejected).

Key options (all :data:`~supy.scm.SCM_PARAM_DEFAULTS` keys may be
overridden as keyword arguments):

- ``stable_fn`` — 0 sharp cut-off (GABLS1-faithful), 1 long tail
  (multi-season runs).
- ``city_length`` — upstream fetch for the ventilation time scale
  [m]; default 15 km.
- ``obs_anchor_tau`` — synoptic anchor time scale [s]; 0 (off) for
  episodes, 86400 for the rural companion in multi-season runs.
- ``radiative_cooling`` — clear-sky longwave divergence [K |day^-1|];
  default 2.
- ``snapshot_every_h`` — return hourly :math:`\theta(z)` and
  :math:`q(z)` profiles (feeds :func:`~supy.scm.make_background`).

Limitations
===========

- One column is one city: horizontal heterogeneity, sea breezes and
  synoptic change enter only through the prescribed radiation and the
  ventilation term.
- The ventilating background is modelled (a rural companion run), not
  observed, and the advective time scale uses a single city-length
  scale.
- Entrainment at the convective boundary-layer top is
  under-represented; afternoon mixed-layer warming may be slightly
  underestimated.
- Wind is constrained by observations; momentum is not freely
  prognostic in the current coupled configuration.
- The synoptic anchor draws on whatever observations the forcing file
  carries — at an urban site this makes the rural reference
  conservative.

Provenance
==========

The physics was developed and validated through a pure-Python reference
implementation, then ported line-for-line into the Fortran core and
pinned by cross-backend regression (air-temperature agreement within
0.06 K over a six-hour coupled window; skill against observations
statistically indistinguishable). The reference implementation —
including its full test suite (exact conservation, Monin--Obukhov
log-law recovery, slab analytic solutions, GABLS1 ranges) — was retired
once the port was pinned, and remains available in the repository
history (commits ``96513b82f``, ``2ca25d90f``, ``dc939564a``,
``7bcf157f0``). The archived metrics in ``test/fixtures/scm/`` and the
figures under ``docs/source/assets/img/scm/`` carry that lineage.
